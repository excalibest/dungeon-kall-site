Tales from the Table

Memorable moments from my games that showcase the magic of tabletop RPGs.

The Stamp Heist (Electric Bastionland)

In one unforgettable one‑shot, a ragtag team found themselves £10,000 in debt to the Stamp Appreciation Society. With no time to spare, they hatched a plan to steal a collection of ultra‑rare stamps displayed at a gala hosted by Lady Gazelle – a literal gazelle and stamp tycoon. The party included a lawyer, a washed‑up college jock, an unsuccessful dog breeder and a family of six siblings spanning multiple timelines. Armed with makeshift weapons, exploding cigars and a surprising amount of improvisation, they infiltrated the party, battled sentient coins and ninjas, and ultimately made off with the coveted Midnight Noir stamp. The mission was chaotic, hilarious and completely improvised – a testament to how much fun you can have with an open mind and good company.

The Fall of Bhaal (Dungeons & Dragons 5E)

In a long‑running D&D campaign, my players faced Bhaal – the god of murder – after he absorbed his brothers and built a world‑spanning cult. The final confrontation lasted only 54 seconds in‑game, but the table exploded with tension and excitement as heroes unleashed their abilities. It remains one of my most satisfying finales and an example of how climactic combat can deliver a truly epic payoff.
