Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about booking and playing with me.

Is this beginner friendly?

Absolutely! Most of the players I work with are trying tabletop RPGs for the first time. I spent over a year running walk‑in games at a local game store, so I’m very comfortable guiding newcomers through the rules and ensuring everyone has fun.

Where do sessions take place?

Sessions are typically hosted in the client’s home, but I’m happy to meet at a neutral venue like a library room or local game store. We’ll pick whatever space makes your group most comfortable.

What do I need to bring?

Nothing except your enthusiasm! I provide miniatures, lights, speakers, books, pencils, character sheets and dice. If you have favourite dice or props you’d like to use, feel free to bring them along.

Do you help with character creation?

Yes. For campaigns we hold a free Session 0 to create characters together. I can also supply pre‑generated characters tailored to your group’s preferences. Some systems have great online tools we can use to speed things along.

How do I schedule and pay?

The easiest way is to use the Calendly link on the Schedule page. You can also contact me via email. Payment can be made through any mainstream digital service (PayPal, Venmo, Cash App, etc.) or by cash/check at the session.

What’s your cancellation policy?

Cancellations are free up to three days before your session. If you cancel within 24 hours without a good reason, you’re eligible for a 50% refund. Emergencies and extenuating circumstances are always fully refunded – no questions asked.

Do you run online sessions?

I currently specialise in in‑person games, but I’m open to online sessions depending on the inquiry. Feel free to ask.

Do you offer long‑term campaigns?

Yes! I love developing long‑running stories. For weekly, bi‑weekly or monthly campaigns, please email me to discuss scheduling and pricing.

What systems do you run?

I currently run Dungeons & Dragons 5E and 2024, Pathfinder 2E Remastered, Electric Bastionland and Mythic Bastionland. I can learn or relearn Blades in the Dark, Mouse Ritter or Panic at the Dojo quickly, and I’m willing to explore Starfinder, Call of Cthulhu, Fabula Ultima, Daggerheart, Cyberpunk and more on request.

Do you provide official modules?

Most of my campaigns are home‑brew adventures crafted for your group, but I’m happy to run published modules if there’s a specific story you’d like to play.

Are sessions suitable for children or families?

Yes. I tailor sessions to suit groups of any age. Participants under 18 must have a parent or legal guardian’s permission to play. For children under 13, a parent or guardian is required to be present at the table, ideally as part of the group. This helps ensure everyone has a safe and enjoyable experience.

What safety and conduct guidelines do you have at the table?

Creating a respectful, inclusive environment is a top priority. Before every session we review “Lines and Veils” – discussing content you want to keep off‑limits or fade to black – and agree on a safety word like “button” that anyone can use to pause or adjust the scene. This conversation can also take place over email prior to the session.

During play I ask everyone to observe a few simple guidelines:

  • Respect your fellow players and the game world; no harassment, discrimination or bullying.
  • Be mindful of the comfort levels of others. Avoid graphic or potentially triggering content unless the group has agreed it’s okay.
  • Keep table talk focused; give everyone a chance to speak and participate.
  • Maintain confidentiality. Stories shared at the table stay at the table unless everyone agrees otherwise.
  • Follow all house rules and local laws regarding behaviour, substances and appropriateness.

If something makes you uncomfortable during play, you can say the safety word or speak to me privately at any time. I’ll pause and adjust the scene to make sure everyone feels safe and heard.
